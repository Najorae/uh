MUSIC STEM SOCIAL JUSTICE HOBBIES



STEM


My dad is a teacher and has a company where he integrates science with hip hop so I’ve been surrounded by science since I was young! We have done shows together performing about the human body and have recorded similar projects years later.

Math was always my favorite subject in school. I enjoy solving puzzles and figuring out how to go through the motions to arrive at the right answer, yet later I understood more how it could be applied to almost all areas of life.

Getting into coding mid-high school, I found my meticulous nature and puzzle solving skills came to use. Interestingly the skills reminded of me of those I used mixing and engineering audio which I had started learning around the same time.

I wish to explore STEM more as I haven't delved as deep as I'd like. I've done programs with UC Berkeley SWE, a stressful 14 hour math competition, months of codecademy, a scratch game, and a lot of teaching myself things on my own. Since I started I've dabbled with C#, Javascript, Python, C++, HTML & CSS and C going on to Girls Who Code workshops and 'taking' CS50!

On the side I love a good big unsolvable math problem or quantum psychics video that I pretend to understand and have found a curiosity in learning how I could integrate STEM with social justice partly inspired by my environmental science teacher.



UC Berkeley SWE:


UC Berkeley SWE's 10 week program was fun and informative! We learned about multiple fields on engineering and engaged with current students. I worked on a one week sustainable tiny home project on autocad: Autocad Presentation. I also enjoyed the one-day mini U hands-on events.





Girls Who Code:


GWC expanded the world of coding some with more projects as I'd previously taught myself on codecademy yet without many implications. Being on zooms in a team felt like a nice little community and it got me to want to learn more and take CS50!
